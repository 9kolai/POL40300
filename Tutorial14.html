Second part: Come up with a simple research quesiton and analyse it.

Remember that for the exam you will have to write a similar report.

  • An open question and some data for you to work with.
  • The report should include the following three elements:
    1. Description of a research design: How would you explore a specific research question?
    2. Reporting and interpretation of findings from some (simple) data analysis (of your choice).
    3. A brief discussion of strengths and weaknesses of your study design. (can be structured as bullet points)
  • Justify your choices!
  • Any use of methods from class will satisfy, but more advanced methods will recieve higher marks (if applied correctly).
  • The report should be concise and to the point!
  • (long essay style discussions will not give you an advantage)
  • You will not be marked on your writing skills, as long as the points you are making are clear.

Marking of written report

Points Description
20-19 Outstanding use of complex methods in a relevant way with no mistakes.
18-17 Very good use of methods and convincing justifications.
16-15 Good use of methods and convincing justifications.
14-13 Good use of methods with no major mistakes.
12-11 Good use of methods, but with some mistakes.
10-9 Satisfactory, but with mistakes and/or incomplete parts.
0-8 Incomplete in substantial parts with essential mistakes.

  • Important that you include all three parts so your report is not incomplete!
  • Use of methods and justifications should be your main focus.

Exam structure

90 minutes closed book

  • The exam will consist of two parts:
    1. A number of concrete questions/tasks that needs to be answered in one or two paragraphs.
    2. An open question that requires you to do some (simple) analysis with data provided and write a short report.
  • You can recieve a maximum of 20 points for each part (so 40 points in total)
  • And you need 21 points to pass.

Exam submission

Consists of two documents:

  1. A written document: This is where you answer all the exam questions and write your report.
  2. An R script (.R extension): This is where you document how you have got the results for your answers and written report.

Exam question examples

There will be different types of questions:

  1. Theoretical questions.
  2. Change the code.
  3. Interpret code output.
  4. Methodological quesitons.

The quesitons can be about anything we have coverede in lectures, tutorials, or readings during the course. (see also slides from yesterday's lecture for an overview of some of the key concepts.

Methodological question example:

There are two basic types of Machine Learning. Imagine you have a dataset, conssiting of tweets from different political campaign groups. You want to give each of these tweets a score on an ideological scale from very left-wing to very right-wing. Which type of Machine Learning would you use?

(1,5 points)

Theoretical question example:

Grimmer and Stewart (2013) describes four principles for computational text analysis. The first of these states that "All quantitative models of language are wrong - but some are useful."

Give an example of how computational text analysis can be useful.

(3 points)
